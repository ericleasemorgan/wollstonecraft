in modern poetry the understanding and memory often fabricate the pretended effusions of the heart, and romance destroys all simplicity; which, in works of taste, is but a synonymous word for truth. this romantic spirit has extended to our prose, and scattered artificial flowers over the most barren heath; or a mixture of verse and prose producing the strangest incongruities. the turgid bombast of some of your periods fully proves these assertions; for / when the heart speaks we are seldom shocked by hyperbole, or dry raptures.
