it is by thus teaching men from their youth to think, that they will be enabled to recover their liberty; and useful learning is already so far advanced, that nothing can stop it's progress:  i say peremptorily nothing; for this is not the era hesitatingly to add, short of supernatural events. and though the unjustifiable proceeding of the english courts of justice, or rather of the arbitrary chief judge mansfield,d who established it as a law precedent, that the greater the truth the greater the libel, tended materially to prevent the authors of the american war from being attacked for those tyrannical steps, that ultimately tended to stop the progress of knowledge and the dissemination of political truth; yet the clamour which was raised against that unpopular war is a proof, that, if justice slept, liberty of thought had not forsaken the island.
