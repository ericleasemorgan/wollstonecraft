probably the prevailing opinion, that woman was created for man, may have taken / its rise from moses's poetical story;a yet, as very few, it is presumed, who have bestowed any serious thought on the subject, ever supposed that eve was, literally speaking, one of adam's ribs, the deduction must be allowed to fall to the ground; or, only be so far admitted as it proves that man, from the remotest antiquity, found it convenient to exert his strength to subjugate his companion, and his invention to shew that she ought to have her neck bent under the yoke, because [the whole creation was only created for his convenience or] pleasure.
