'the sentiment, that a woman may allow all innocent freedoms, provided her virtue is secure, is both grossly indelicate and dangerous, / and has proved fatal to many of your sex.'b with this opinion i perfectly coincide. a man, or a woman, of any feeling, must always wish to convince a beloved object that it is the caresses of the individual, not the sex, that [are] received and returned with pleasure; and, that the heart, rather than the senses, is moved. without this natural delicacy, love becomes a selfish personal gratification that soon degrades the character.
