women have seldom sufficient serious employment to silence their feelings; a round of little cares, or vain pursuits frittering away all strength of mind and organs, they become naturally only objects of sense.  in short, the whole tenour of female education (the education of society) tends to render the best disposed romantic and inconstant; and the remainder vain and mean. in the present state of society this evil can scarcely be remedied, / i am afraid, in the slightest degree; should a more laudable ambition ever gain ground
