'beauty cannot be acquired by dress, and coquetry is an art not so early and speedily attained. while girls are yet young, however, they are in a capacity to study agreeable / gesture, a pleasing modulation of voice, an easy carriage and behaviour; as well as to take the advantage of gracefully adapting their looks and attitudes to time, place, and occasion. their application, therefore, should not be solely confined to the arts of industry and the needle, when they come to display other talents, whose utility is already apparent.'
