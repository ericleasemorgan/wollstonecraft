taking a view of the different works which have been written on education, lord chesterfield's letters must not be silently passed over. not that i mean to analyze his unmanly, immoral system, or even to cull any of the useful, shrewd remarks which occur in his [epistles]  no, i only mean to make a few reflections on the avowed tendency of them  the art of acquiring an early knowledge / of the world. an art, i will venture to assert, that preys secretly, like the worm in the bud,a on the expanding powers, and turns to poison the generous juices which should mount with vigour in the youthful frame, inspiring warm affections and great resolves.
