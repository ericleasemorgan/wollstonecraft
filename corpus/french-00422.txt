at eight o'clock,c the patrol guard was relieved, at the htel-de-ville, and the multitude pressed on the soldiers to disarm them; redoubling the cry for arms at the moment; and even threatened to set fire to the hall. but, still observing some respect for subordination, they demanded, a little imperiously, it is true, an order, in virtue of which, the citizens might arm themselves to repulse the danger that menaced the capital  and amidst these clamours, several precipitate reports painted, in the most lively colours, this danger.
