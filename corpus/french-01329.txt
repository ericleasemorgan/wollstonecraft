thus sentiments spouted from the lips come oftner from the head than the heart. indeed natural sentiments are only the characters given by the imagination to recollected sensations; but the french, by the continual gratification of their senses, stifle the reveries of their imagination, which always requires to be acted upon by outward objects; and seldom reflecting on their feelings, their sensations are ever lively and transitory; exhaled / by every passing beam, and dissipated by the slightest storm.
