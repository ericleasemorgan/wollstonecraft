 'o how lovely,' exclaims rousseau, speaking of sophia, 'is her ignorance! happy is he who is destined to instruct her! she will never pretend to be the tutor of her husband, but will be content to be his pupil. far from attempting to subject him to her taste, she will accommodate herself to his. she will be more estimable to him, than if she was learned: he will have a pleasure in instructing her.' rousseau's emilius.d
