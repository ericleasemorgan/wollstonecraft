and women grow more modest  till men, curbing a sensual fondness for the sex, or an affectation of manly assurance, more properly speaking, impudence, treat each other with respect  unless appetite or passion [give] the tone, peculiar to it, to their behaviour. i mean even personal respect  the modest respect of humanity, and fellow-feeling  not the libidinous mockery of gallantry, nor the insolent condescension of protectorship.
