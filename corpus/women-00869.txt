for instance, the severest sarcasms have been levelled against the sex, and they have been ridiculed for repeating 'a set of phrases learnt by rote,'b when nothing could be more natural, considering the education they receive, and that their 'highest praise is to obey, unargued'c  the will of man. if they [be] not allowed to have reason sufficient to govern their own conduct  why, all they learn  must be learned by rote! and when all their ingenuity is called forth to adjust their dress, 'a passion for a scarlet coat,'d is so natural, that it never surprised me; and, allowing pope's summary of their character to be just, 'that every woman is at heart a rake,'e why should they be bitterly censured for seeking a congenial mind, and preferring a rake to a man of sense?
