the being who can govern itself has nothing to fear in life; but if any thing [be] dearer than its own respect, the price must be paid to the last farthing. virtue, like every thing valuable, must be loved for herself alone; or she will not take up her abode with us. she will not impart that peace, 'which passeth understanding',b when she is merely made the stilts of reputation; and respected, with pharisaical exactness, because 'honesty is the best policy.'
