such were the grievances!  such the impositions, 'that, taken together, levied a tax on the kingdom,' says rabaud, 'which the imagination is afraid to calculate.'b this body of men we may consider as constituting france, till the great bulk of the people, who were slaves and dwarfs, bursting their shackles and rising in stature, suddenly appeared with the dignity and pretentions of human beings: yes; with the same feelings; or perhaps stronger, because more natural; and claiming equal rights with those nobles, who, like the giants of old, were only great by the courtesy of the imagination. who is so callous to the interest of humanity as to say it was not a noble regeneration?
