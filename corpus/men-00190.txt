health can only be secured by temperance; but is it easy to persuade a man to live on plain food even to recover his health, who has been accustomed to fare sumptuously every day? can a man relish the simple food of friendship, who has been habitually pampered by flattery? and when the blood boils, and the senses meet allurements on every side, will knowledge be pursued on account of its abstract beauty? no; it is well known that talents are only to be unfolded by industry, and / that we must have made some advances, led by an inferior motive, before we discover that they are their own reward.
