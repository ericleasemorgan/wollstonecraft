sailors, the naval gentlemen, come under the same description, only their vices assume a different and grosser cast. they are more positively indolent, when not discharging the ceremonials of their station; whilst the insignificant fluttering of soldiers may be termed active idleness. more confined to the society of men, the former acquire a fondness for humour and mischievous tricks; whilst the latter, mixing frequently with well-bred women, catch a sentimental cant.  but mind is equally out of the question, / whether they indulge the horse-laugh, or polite simper.
